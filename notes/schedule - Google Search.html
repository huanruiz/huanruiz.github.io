      Phrases
      ahead of schedule
      earlier than planned or expected. "work finished an astonishing twelve days ahead of schedule"
      behind schedule
      later than planned or expected. "I'm behind schedule as it is"
      on schedule
      on time; as planned or expected. "the filming was still on schedule"
      Origin
      late Middle English (in the sense ‘scroll, explanatory note, appendix’): from Old French cedule, from late Latin schedula ‘slip of paper’, diminutive of scheda, from Greek skhedē ‘papyrus leaf’. The verb dates from the mid 19th century.
